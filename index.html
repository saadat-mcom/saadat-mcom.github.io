About Me

Engineer by trade, educator by passion.

I am a Senior Software Engineer with extensive experience designing and delivering complex embedded, mobile and cloud systems across the mining, transit, and telecommunications industries. My work spans the full stack — from bare-metal firmware on Raspberry Pi hardware to enterprise Android applications and AWS cloud back-ends.

Alongside my industry career I have held lecturer positions at TAFE NSW and UniKL, delivering units in computing and engineering while supervising Masters and PhD candidates. I bring both the rigour of academic research and the pragmatism of production-grade engineering to every project.

I am actively seeking a new senior engineering or technical lead role where I can drive impact at scale — ideally in autonomous systems, connected vehicles, cloud-native platforms or AI-enabled solutions.

Engineering Portfolio

Selected projects from my industry career

HERE Navigation & GTFS Platform

Enterprise transit navigation solution integrating HERE Maps SDK with GTFS real-time feeds. Delivers turn-by-turn guidance, route handles, stop matching and live journey tracking for public transport operators at scale.

AndroidHERE SDK GTFSJava REST API

Remote Fleet Telemetry Platform

Edge-to-cloud telematics system for Komatsu mining equipment. Raspberry Pi edge nodes read J1939/CAN Bus data from heavy machinery, transmit over cellular to an AWS back-end providing real-time dashboards and predictive diagnostics.

Raspberry PiCAN Bus / J1939 AWS IoTPython MQTT

AI People Counting Platform

Computer-vision system deployed on embedded hardware to count and track foot-traffic in real time. Outputs analytics feeds consumed by venue management and occupancy planning tools.

Embedded LinuxOpenCV PythonComputer Vision IoT

Motorola Radio Communicator System

Android and Raspberry Pi solution enabling software-controlled operation of Motorola professional radios via XCMP/XNL protocols. Provides fleet-wide push-to-talk, channel management and remote diagnostics.
